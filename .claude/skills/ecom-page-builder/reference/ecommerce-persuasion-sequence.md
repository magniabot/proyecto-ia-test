# Ecommerce Persuasion Sequence Reference

Agent-readable reference defining the section hierarchies for Dedicated Ecommerce LPs and Product Pages. The core principle: **proof comes before benefits** in ecommerce because the product image already communicates the value proposition visually.

---

## Decision Gate: Dedicated LP vs Product Page

Before building, select the right page type.

| Use a Dedicated LP when... | Use a Product Page when... |
|---------------------------|------------------------------|
| Running a specific promotion or seasonal offer | Traffic is from Shopping PLAs (expects a product page) |
| Single product or bundle with a clear, focused offer | Visitors need to browse variants or related products |
| Cold traffic from Display/YouTube/Demand Gen | The product page already converts well |
| You want to A/B test messaging independently | Warm traffic from branded or high-intent search |
| You control the full page (no platform constraints) | Product has strong reviews that do the selling |

> **Do not use a Dedicated LP for Shopping PLA traffic.** Shopping visitors expect a product page with site navigation. A stripped LP will confuse them and increase bounce rate.

---

## Why Ecommerce Is Different

| Dimension | Lead Gen / SaaS LP | Ecommerce Pages |
|-----------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **What sells** | Copy and promises | Product imagery, reviews, and specs |
| **Hero section** | Headline-first, promise-focused | Product image-first, price visible |
| **Persuasion order** | Benefits → Social Proof | Social Proof → Benefits (proof first) |
| **Trust signals** | Company credentials, case studies | Reviews, return policy, shipping, security |
| **CTA placement** | After trust is fully built | Product page: Position 3 (early) |
| **Navigation** | Always stripped | Dedicated LP: stripped / Product page: included |
| **Urgency mechanics** | Limited spots, deadlines | Stock scarcity, shipping cutoffs |

---

## Five Ecommerce Conversion Principles

| # | Principle | Application |
|---|-----------|------------|
| 1 | **The product is the pitch** | Images, reviews, and specs sell. Copy supports but doesn't lead. |
| 2 | **One job per page** | Each page type has exactly one goal. Optimize for that goal only. |
| 3 | **Trust is transactional** | Reviews, return policies, shipping info matter more than company credentials. |
| 4 | **Friction compounds** | Every extra click, surprise charge, or form field costs revenue. |
| 5 | **The funnel is the page system** | No single page converts alone. The buyer journey across page types IS the conversion mechanism. |

## Dedicated Ecommerce LP: 7-Section Sequence

The Ecommerce Persuasion Sequence for no-navigation, paid traffic landing pages.

| # | Section | Visitor Question | Key Elements |
|---|---------|-----------------|-------------|
| 1 | **Product Showcase** | What's being offered? | Product image/video as hero (not headline-first). Price visible immediately. Key differentiator tagline. Primary CTA. |
| 2 | **Customer Evidence** | Does it actually work? | Reviews, UGC, before/after, aggregate rating. Proof comes early because the product image already established "what it is." |
| 3 | **Product Benefits** | How will this help me? | Feature-to-outcome translations, supported by the proof above. Benefits land harder after proof. |
| 4 | **The Details** | Tell me more. | Specs, comparison, what's in the box, how it works. For detail-oriented buyers. |
| 5 | **Purchase Confidence** | What if it doesn't work out? | Returns, shipping, sizing, FAQ. Transactional concerns, not conceptual ones. |
| 6 | **Act Now** | Why not wait? | Stock scarcity, shipping cutoffs, promotion deadlines. Legitimate reasons only. |
| 7 | **Complete Your Purchase** | Let me buy. | Value recap + price + CTA + express checkout + guarantee reminder. |

### Why Proof Before Benefits

In Lead Gen/SaaS, the hero makes a promise, benefits expand on it, and social proof validates it afterward. The visitor needs to understand the offer conceptually before evaluating it.

In ecommerce, the product image immediately communicates what's being offered. A visitor sees running shoes, they know it's running shoes. The next question isn't "What do these do?" but "Do they actually hold up?" Customer evidence answers this directly, so when benefits follow, the visitor is already primed to believe the claims.

### Section Emphasis (Dedicated LP)

All sections default to Heavy emphasis for Dedicated LPs. Urgency is conditional on availability.

| Section | Default Emphasis | Notes |
|---------|-----------------|-------|
| Product Showcase | Full | Always full — product-as-hero, price above fold |
| Customer Evidence | Heavy | Critical — proof before benefits |
| Product Benefits | Heavy | Feature-to-outcome cards |
| The Details | Moderate | Specs table, comparison, what's in the box |
| Purchase Confidence | Heavy | Returns, shipping, FAQ |
| Act Now | Conditional | Heavy if urgency exists; Skip if none |
| Complete Your Purchase | Full | Value recap, price, CTA, express checkout |

### CTA Placement (Dedicated LP)

Minimum 4 CTAs:
1. In Product Showcase (hero)
2. After Customer Evidence
3. After Purchase Confidence (FAQ/guarantee)
4. In Complete Your Purchase (final section)

## Product Page: 7-Section Hierarchy

The standard product page with site navigation, used for Shopping PLAs and organic traffic.

| # | Section | Visitor Question | Key Elements |
|---|---------|-----------------|-------------|
| 1 | **Product Identity** | What is this? | Image/video gallery, title, price, variant selectors |
| 2 | **Offer Stack** | What do I get for this price? | Pricing, shipping info, return policy, availability |
| 3 | **Add-to-Cart Action** | How do I buy it? | CTA button (most prominent element), quantity, express pay |
| 4 | **Social Proof** | Do others like it? | Star rating, review count, customer photos, review sorting |
| 5 | **Product Details** | Tell me more. | Benefit-first description, specs table, FAQ |
| 6 | **Cross-sell** | What else? | Frequently bought together, related products, recently viewed |
| 7 | **Trust Reinforcement** | Can I trust this? | Return policy (expanded), secure checkout badge, contact, certifications |

### Why CTA at Position 3

On a product page, the add-to-cart button comes at position 3, above social proof and details. The product itself is the hero: images and price do the selling. A visitor who sees the product, understands the offer, and wants it should be able to act immediately without scrolling past paragraphs of persuasion.

Social proof, product details, and trust reinforcement appear below the CTA for visitors who need more convincing. They serve as a safety net, not a prerequisite.

### Section Emphasis (Product Page)

| Section | Default Emphasis | Notes |
|---------|-----------------|-------|
| Product Identity | Full | Gallery minimum 5 images, title, price, variants |
| Offer Stack | Full | Price + shipping + returns near CTA |
| Add-to-Cart Action | Full | Most prominent element, sticky on mobile |
| Social Proof | Heavy | Reviews, customer photos, sortable |
| Product Details | Moderate | Benefit-first description, specs, FAQ |
| Cross-sell | Moderate | Frequently bought together + related |
| Trust Reinforcement | Light | Return policy, security badge, contact |

### CTA Placement (Product Page)

- Primary CTA at position 3 (Add-to-Cart Action section)
- Sticky bottom bar on mobile when scrolling past main CTA
- Express pay buttons (Apple Pay, Google Pay, Shop Pay) below main CTA

## Messaging Angle by Traffic Source

For Dedicated LPs, select the messaging angle based on traffic source:

| Traffic Source | Awareness Level | Messaging Angle |
|---------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Branded search | High awareness | Lead with offer/price, light persuasion |
| Generic product search | Medium awareness | Lead with product benefits, social proof heavy |
| Display/YouTube | Low awareness | Lead with problem/transformation, full persuasion |
| Demand Gen | Low-medium | Lead with lifestyle/aspiration, visual-heavy |
| Retargeting | High (already visited) | Lead with urgency/offer, address remaining objections |
