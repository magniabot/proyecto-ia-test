# Awareness Stages & Page Hierarchy Reference

Agent-readable reference combining awareness-level strategy with the 7-section landing page hierarchy blueprint.

---

## The 5 Awareness Stages

| Stage | What they know | What they need | Temperature |
|-------|---------------|----------------|-------------|
| **Unaware** | Doesn't know the problem exists | Problem identification | Cold |
| **Problem Aware** | Knows the problem, not the solutions | Problem validation + consequence | Cold |
| **Solution Aware** | Knows solutions exist, evaluating options | Your unique approach | Warm |
| **Product Aware** | Knows your product, comparing to alternatives | Specific benefits + differentiation | Warm |
| **Most Aware** | Ready to buy, needs the right offer | Offer details + urgency | Hot |

| Traffic Source | Typical Stage | LP Approach |
|---------------|--------------|-------------|
| **Brand Search** | Most Aware | Direct offer, short-form, streamlined CTA |
| **Competitor Search** | Product Aware | Differentiators, heavy proof, comparison |

| **Display/YouTube** | Unaware → Problem Aware | Problem identification, content-heavy |
| **Remarketing** | Product Aware → Most Aware | Value boosters, risk removal, urgency |

## Message Strategy by Stage

### Unaware
- **Focus:** Problem identification — make them aware something is wrong
- **Tone:** Informative, eye-opening
- **Lead angle:** Problem/Pain (educational framing)
- **CTA type:** "Learn more" / "Discover"

### Problem Aware
- **Focus:** Problem articulation — validate frustration, show consequences
- **Tone:** Empathetic, understanding
- **Lead angle:** Problem/Pain (validation framing)
- **CTA type:** "Discover solution" / "See how"

### Solution Aware
- **Focus:** Your unique approach — differentiate from other solutions
- **Tone:** Authoritative, differentiating
- **Lead angle:** USP / Value Proposition
- **CTA type:** "See how it works" / "Compare"

### Product Aware
- **Focus:** Specific benefits & features — why you beat alternatives
- **Tone:** Confident, specific
- **Lead angle:** USP + Social Proof
- **CTA type:** "Compare options" / "See features"

### Most Aware
- **Focus:** Offer details & urgency — give them the deal, remove friction
- **Tone:** Direct, urgent
- **Lead angle:** Value Proposition + Risk Removal + Urgency
- **CTA type:** "Get started now" / "Buy now" / "Start free trial"

## Angle Priority by Stage

| Angle | Unaware | Problem Aware | Solution Aware | Product Aware | Most Aware |
|-------|---------|--------------|----------------|--------------|------------|
| **Problem/Pain** | LEAD | LEAD | Include | Light | Skip |
| **Value Proposition** | Light | Include | LEAD | LEAD | LEAD |
| **USPs** | Skip | Light | Include | LEAD | LEAD |
| **Value Boosters** | Skip | Skip | Light | Include | LEAD |
| **Social Proof** | Skip | Light | Include | LEAD | Include |
| **Risk Removal** | Skip | Skip | Light | Include | LEAD |

**LEAD** = Primary emphasis, multiple placements | **Include** = Present | **Light** = Brief mention | **Skip** = Omit

## The 7-Section Hierarchy Blueprint

| # | Section | Visitor's Question | Goal | Position |
|---|---------|-------------------|------|----------|
| 1 | **Hero** | What exactly is being offered? | Capture attention, communicate core value, establish relevance | Above the fold |
| 2 | **Benefits** | How will this help me? | Show outcomes and transformation, connect to desires | First scroll |
| 3 | **Trust/Authority** | Why should I believe you? | Establish credibility through credentials, certifications, expertise | After benefits |
| 4 | **Social Proof** | What do others say about this? | Provide third-party validation through testimonials, case studies, stats | After trust |
| 5 | **Objection Handling** | What if it doesn't work? | Remove mental friction, address doubts, provide guarantees | Middle of page |
| 6 | **Urgency/Scarcity** | Why should I act now? | Create legitimate FOMO, time or supply pressure | Before final CTA |
| 7 | **Call to Action** | What should I do next? | Provide clear direction, reinforce value, trigger conversion | Repeated throughout + final |

**Psychological progression:** Trust before proof, proof before pressure. Establish credibility → validate with others → then create urgency. Pressure on an unconvinced visitor causes bounces, not conversions.

## Copywriting Framework by Awareness

| Awareness Level | Best Framework | Structure | Why |
|----------------|---------------|-----------|-----|
| **Unaware** | AIDA | Attention → Interest → Desire → Action | Need to capture attention and build interest from zero |
| **Problem Aware** | PAS | Problem → Agitation → Solution | They know the problem, agitate it then present solution |
| **Solution Aware** | AIDA or BAB | Before → After → Bridge | Show transformation from current state to desired state |
| **Product Aware** | Hook-Story-Offer | Hook → Story → Offer | Narrative differentiation, why you vs competitors |
| **Most Aware** | Direct Offer | Offer → Proof → CTA | Skip the story, lead with the deal |

Frameworks are not rigid. Combine elements across frameworks (e.g., PAS opening with AIDA middle).

## Section Detail Reference

### Section 1: Hero
- **Required:** Primary headline (under 10 words), sub-headline (under 2 sentences), visual element, primary CTA
- **Optional:** Microcopy near CTA, secondary CTA, anchor navigation
- **Rule:** 80% of visitors decide to stay or leave based on the hero alone
- **Key mistakes:** Vague headlines, generic stock photos, no CTA above fold, feature-focused headlines, no message match

### Section 2: Benefits
- **Three angles:** Functional (time/cost/ease), Business (efficiency/resources), Personal (stress/confidence)
- **Display patterns:** Icon + headline + description grid, before/after comparison, numbered list, feature-benefit pairs
- **Rule:** Benefits focus on outcomes, not features. Use "So what?" technique until you reach the emotional benefit

### Section 3: Trust/Authority
- **Types:** Institutional (certifications, compliance badges) and Expertise (awards, credentials, results)
- **By vertical:** Regulated industries → institutional trust first. Services/consulting → expertise trust first
- **Rule:** Trust must be established before social proof is effective

### Section 4: Social Proof
- **Types:** Testimonials, case studies, statistical proof, expert endorsements, third-party validation
- **Testimonial formula:** Specific result + before state + after state + recommendation
- **Rule:** Real, verifiable, specific. Full names and photos. Match to visitor segment

### Section 5: Objection Handling
- **Techniques:** Guarantee statements, FAQ sections, value framing, process clarifications, targeted testimonials
- **Common objections:** Price, value, implementation, timing, trust, fit
- **Rule:** Address top 3 objections proactively before they become conversion barriers

### Section 6: Urgency/Scarcity
- **Urgency:** Limited time offers, deadline-driven events, progressive deadlines, time-sensitive bonuses
- **Scarcity:** Limited quantity, exclusive access, founding member pricing
- **Rule:** Only legitimate techniques. Fake scarcity destroys trust permanently. Place after value and trust

### Section 7: Call to Action
- **Principles:** Value-focused, specific, first-person, contrasting, repeated
- **Placement:** After hero, after benefits, after social proof, after objection handling, after urgency, final section
- **Microcopy patterns:** Risk removal, social proof, speed/ease, urgency, value reinforcement
