# Goal Quality Checklist

Used by D10-D14 diagnostics to validate goal and KPI setup in business.md.

## Business Goals

- [ ] Business goal is documented in writing
- [ ] Goal is Specific (focused on one outcome)
- [ ] Goal is Measurable (has a quantifiable target)
- [ ] Goal is Achievable (grounded in historical data and market capacity)
- [ ] Goal is Relevant (aligned with broader business strategy)
- [ ] Goal is Time-bound (has a deadline)
- [ ] Primary goal type identified: growth or efficiency
- [ ] Secondary goal type identified as guardrail
- [ ] Stakeholder has approved the business goal

## Google Ads Goal Translation

- [ ] Google Ads goal directly supports the business goal
- [ ] Translation accounts for multi-channel contributions
- [ ] Google Ads goal uses in-platform metrics
- [ ] Goal is actionable within Google Ads capabilities
- [ ] Assumptions are documented (channel contribution %, conversion rates, etc.)
- [ ] Google Ads goal is SMART

## KPI Selection

- [ ] Primary KPIs directly measure the Google Ads goal
- [ ] Secondary KPIs (guardrails) prevent damage from primary goal pursuit
- [ ] Guardrails have specific numeric thresholds (not "keep an eye on")
- [ ] Diagnostic KPIs identified for troubleshooting (not used as targets)
- [ ] No vanity metrics in primary or secondary KPIs
- [ ] KPIs are measurable within Google Ads or connected systems

## Growth-Focused Accounts

- [ ] Primary KPIs include conversion volume, conversion value, or revenue
- [ ] Guardrail KPIs include minimum ROAS and/or maximum CPA
- [ ] Guardrail thresholds derived from unit economics (not arbitrary)
- [ ] Impression share tracked as diagnostic KPI

## Efficiency-Focused Accounts

- [ ] Primary KPIs include CPA, ROAS, or cost per qualified lead
- [ ] Guardrail KPIs include minimum conversion volume and/or value
- [ ] Volume guardrails ensure campaigns don't dry out (min 30 conversions/month per campaign)
- [ ] Competitive metrics tracked to detect impression share erosion

## Goal Viability Validation

- [ ] Unit economics validated: targets above break-even thresholds
- [ ] Budget viability confirmed: budget supports enough clicks to hit conversion targets
- [ ] No conflicting objectives (growth and efficiency not both maximized)
- [ ] Business capacity confirmed: team can handle projected volume

## Red Flags (any = revisit goals)

- Goals assume linear growth with increased spend
- Goals require simultaneous growth and efficiency maximization
- Budget insufficient for stated targets
- Timeline unrealistic given historical performance
- Technical limitations block measurement of success KPIs
