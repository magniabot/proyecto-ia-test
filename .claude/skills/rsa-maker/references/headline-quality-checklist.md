# Headline Quality Checklist — Reference

Run after composing all headlines. Validates constraints, quality, and angle coverage.

## Constraints

- [ ] Each headline <= 30 characters
- [ ] Most headlines 25-30 characters (space utilized)

## Standalone Validity

- [ ] Each headline makes sense on its own
- [ ] No headline requires another headline to be understood
- [ ] DKI defaults are strong standalone phrases (not generic)
- [ ] No awkward fragments or broken phrases

## Non-Duplication

- [ ] No two headlines express the same claim in different words
- [ ] No headline duplicates sitelink text verbatim
- [ ] No headline duplicates callout text verbatim

## Quality

- [ ] No generic filler ("best", "quality", "leading") without specifics
- [ ] Quantified claims are believable and defensible
- [ ] Searcher-focused, not self-focused ("We are...")
- [ ] No vague claims that competitors could equally make

## Angle Coverage

- [ ] Relevance Anchor exists (DKI or static)
- [ ] Problem/Pain headline exists (where appropriate for traffic temperature)
- [ ] Value Proposition headline exists
- [ ] CTA headline exists
- [ ] Trust (Social Proof/Authority) or Risk Removal headline exists

## Modifiers (If Used)

- [ ] Urgency language appears in <=2 headlines
- [ ] Price headlines only used where conversion intent supports
